# Pull Day Routine

A comprehensive back and biceps workout designed to build a strong, wide back and powerful arms. This routine emphasizes horizontal and vertical pulling patterns.

## Workout Overview
- **Duration**: 50-70 minutes
- **Frequency**: Once or twice per week (depending on split)
- **Equipment**: Pull-up bar, dumbbells, barbell, cables, machines
- **Intensity**: Moderate to High
- **Focus**: Lats, back thickness, biceps, rear delts

---

## Exercise Selection Guide

### A. Primary Pulling Movement (Pick One)
Vertical or horizontal pulling emphasis:

| Exercise | Best For | Sets × Reps | Load |
|----------|----------|------------|------|
| Pull-ups (Weighted) | Lat width, upper back strength | 4-5 × 4-8 | Progressive load |
| Seated Dumbbell Row | Back thickness, controlled | 4 × 6-10 | Heavy-Moderate |
| Barbell Row | Overall back strength, thickness | 4-5 × 4-8 | Heavy (70-85% 1RM) |
| Machine Lat Pulldown | Accessible, high volume | 3 × 8-12 | Moderate |

### B. Secondary Pulling Exercise (Pick One or Two)
Add complementary pulling pattern:

| Exercise | Target | Sets × Reps | Rest |
|----------|--------|------------|------|
| Pull-ups (Bodyweight) | Lat width, endurance | 3-4 × 8-12 | 60-90s |
| Assisted Pull-up Machine | Accessible strength | 3 × 8-12 | 60-90s |
| Chest-Supported Row | Back, less lower back stress | 3 × 8-12 | 90s |
| Machine Lat Pulldown | High reps, isolation | 3 × 10-15 | 60-90s |

### C. Back Isolation (Pick One or Two)
Target deeper back muscles:

| Exercise | Style | Sets × Reps | Rest |
|----------|-------|------------|------|
| Reverse Fly | Rear delts, upper back | 3 × 10-15 | 60s |
| Lat Isolation Machine | Single-arm control, high reps | 3 × 12-15 | 60s |

### D. Bicep Work (Pick One or Two)
Direct arm development:

| Exercise | Style | Sets × Reps | Rest |
|----------|-------|------------|------|
| Barbell or Dumbbell Curl | Heavy, compound arm | 3-4 × 6-10 | 90s |
| Dumbbell Hammer Curl | Neutral grip, brachialis | 3 × 10-12 | 60s |
| Machine Bicep Curl | Isolation, controlled | 3 × 10-15 | 60s |

### E. Accessory Finisher (Optional)
Light work to finish:

| Exercise | Reps | Rest |
|----------|------|------|
| Face Pulls | 15-20 × 2-3 sets | Minimal |
| Dumbbell Shrug | 10-15 × 2 sets | 60s |

## How to Log This Workout

Create a new file for each exercise performed in the `exercises/` folder.

### File Naming Convention
```
YYYY-MM-DD ExerciseName.md
```

Example filenames:
- `2025-10-30 Barbell Row.md`
- `2025-10-30 Weighted Pull-ups.md`
- `2025-10-30 Dumbbell Curl.md`
- `2025-10-30 Reverse Fly.md`

### YAML Frontmatter
Use this template for each exercise:

```yaml
---
exercise-name: Barbell Row
exercise-date: YYYY-MM-DD
muscle-group: Back
weight: 225
reps: 5
sets: 5
effort: 4
workout-type: Upper Body Pull
notes: "Strong contraction, maintained form throughout"
---
```

### Key Properties
- **exercise-name**: The specific exercise performed
- **exercise-date**: Today's date in YYYY-MM-DD format
- **muscle-group**: Primary muscle (Back, Lats, Biceps, Rear Delts, Traps, etc.)
- **weight**: Weight used in pounds (0 for bodyweight)
- **reps**: Reps per set (your heaviest/most difficult set)
- **sets**: Number of sets completed
- **effort**: Intensity level (1=easy, 5=maximum effort)
- **notes**: Feel in the muscle, form observations, grip position

## Progressive Overload for Back & Biceps

1. **Add Weight**: Increase barbell/dumbbell weight by 5-10 lbs
2. **Increase Reps**: Progress from 5 → 6 → 8 reps before adding weight
3. **Add Reps to Pull-ups**: Unweighted pull-ups by 1-2 reps
4. **Decrease Rest**: Reduce between-set rest by 15-30 seconds
5. **Add Volume**: Increase sets from 3 → 4 sets
6. **Add Weighted Resistance**: Progress from bodyweight to 10 lbs, 25 lbs, etc.

## Back Development Principles

### For Lat Width
- Focus on wide-grip pull-ups and lat pulldowns
- Emphasize the stretch feeling at bottom of movement
- Use controlled, full range of motion

### For Back Thickness
- Emphasize rows (barbell, dumbbell, machine)
- Shorter range of motion with heavy weight
- Focus on squeezing shoulder blades together
- Retract scapula completely

### For Bicep Development
- Emphasize eccentric (lowering) portion
- 2-3 second descent
- Full range of motion
- Vary grip (narrow, wide, neutral)

## Form & Safety Checklist

### Barbell Row Form
- [ ] Stance: Feet shoulder-width, slight knee bend
- [ ] Grip: Slightly wider than shoulder width
- [ ] Setup: Shoulders slightly in front of bar
- [ ] Pull: Drive chest to bar, not face
- [ ] Elbows: 45° angle from body
- [ ] Core: Braced throughout entire set

### Pull-up Form
- [ ] Grip: Hands slightly wider than shoulders
- [ ] Start: Full arm extension (dead hang)
- [ ] Path: Body moves straight up/down
- [ ] Target: Chin over bar
- [ ] Control: Slow eccentric, explosive concentric

### Curl Form
- [ ] Elbows: Stay at sides, not moving forward
- [ ] Shoulders: Avoid shrugging or swaying
- [ ] Wrist: Neutral, not hyperextended
- [ ] Range: Full extension to full contraction
- [ ] Control: 2 second descent, 1 second pause at top

## Recovery & Nutrition

- **Protein**: 0.8-1g per lb bodyweight daily
- **Hydration**: 3-4L water on pull day
- **Recovery**: 48-72 hours before next back session
- **Stretching**: Lats, biceps, shoulders post-workout
- **Sleep**: 7-9 hours for optimal muscle growth

---

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Not achieving full range of motion** - Sacrifice depth for heavier weight
2. **Using arms instead of back** - Forget about contracting the lats
3. **Jerky, bouncy movements** - Reduce weight, focus on control
4. **Neglecting rear delts** - Add direct reverse fly work
5. **Inconsistent grip width** - Vary grip to hit different back areas
